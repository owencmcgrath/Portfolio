The day that I visited AllSides ↗, the story was about a hockey game where Canadian fans booed the American anthem prior to the game starting due to recent political tensions between the neighboring countries. The three main articles for this Headline Roundup were: from the left, NBC ↗; from the center, Newsweek ↗; and from the right, The New York Post ↗.

Prior to analyzing these articles, it is important to consider my own bias, in which I am incredibly left-leaning, and therefore likely to be biased towards the left-leaning sources. On almost all topics, I agree with liberal politicians and have only ever voted in line with a liberal ideology. Below is my political compass from The Political Compass ↗, which attempts to measure political ideology on a two-dimensional scale. The compass measures political ideology on a left-right axis and a libertarian-authoritarian axis. As is obvious, I sit on the left extreme.

As for the articles themselves, each one spoke about the events leading up to the game as well as during the game. They also discussed the primary reason for the boos, which comes from Trump's attempt to enforce 25% tariffs on Canada, as well as potentially trying to make Canada the "51st state."

Both Newsweek and The New York Post elected to discuss what happened on social media after the game, as well as who was in attendance, with most quotes coming from X users. NBC, on the other hand, elected to focus on the game itself, with no quotes and no focus on social media. All of the sites failed to link to external sources, unless they came from X. If there was linkage, it was to stories that were written on their own site.

The wording and tone of each site varied, but NBC seemed to refrain from words of grandeur, while both Newsweek and The New York Post used more interpretive language to describe the events. For example, The New York Post wrote of "handmade fireworks" to describe the fights during the game or "torrential jeering" to describe the fans' reactions to the National Anthem.

Unfortunately, none of the sites provided any form of verification or sources. While this story can be seen as "light" or unserious, it's important to note that the lack of verification and sources makes it difficult to determine the accuracy of the claims made in the article. Most of the quotes and descriptions were taken from social media, where anyone can post, and posters rarely have to take responsibility for their content.

In conclusion, it seems as though NBC had the most amount of factual reporting and the least amount of bias, but Newsweek was a close second. The New York Post used several metaphors and colorful descriptions on a story that didn't need them. This experiment taught me that each story, no matter how light or unserious it may seem, should be treated with the same level of scrutiny and verification as any other news story. It's important to remember that even seemingly trivial stories can have significant impacts on individuals and society as a whole.
